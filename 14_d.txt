UDP is preferred over TCP in real-time streaming because:

UDP sends packets without waiting for acknowledgements or retransmissions.
This minimizes delay and keeps video playback smooth and real-time.
UDP is connectionless, so there is no
no handshake,no congestion control,
and no packet ordering overhead.
This improves streaming speed.In video streaming, receiving the newest frame quickly is more important than perfectly receiving every old frame.
TCP retransmits lost packets, which introduces buffering and lag.
UDP simply skips lost packets and continues streaming.

Visual Artifact When a UDP Frame is Dropped
When a UDP frame is lostthe receiver misses one video frame,
causing a temporary visual glitch.like-

frame freezing,
image flickering,
missing motion,
sudden jumps in movement,
partial corruption/blocking in compressed streams.

The video usually recovers immediately when the next frame arrives, which is acceptable in real-time applications such as:
